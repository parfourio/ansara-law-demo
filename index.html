Know This

Your Rights After an Arrest

Most people don't know their rights until it's too late. Here's what you need to know immediately.

🤫 You Have the Right to Remain Silent

Anything you say to police can and will be used against you. You are not required to answer questions beyond identifying yourself. Exercise this right immediately and call an attorney.

⚖️ Arrest Is Not Conviction

Being charged with a crime does not make you guilty. The prosecution must prove guilt beyond a reasonable doubt. An experienced defense attorney can challenge evidence, witnesses, and procedure.

📞 You Have the Right to an Attorney

You have the right to have an attorney present before and during any questioning. Invoking this right cannot be held against you. Call The Ansara Law Firm at (954) 761-4011 immediately.

⏰ Time Matters Enormously

Evidence can be lost, witnesses' memories fade, and procedural deadlines close quickly. The sooner you have experienced counsel, the more options are available for your defense.

🔍 Evidence Can Be Challenged

Breathalyzer results, search and seizure procedures, chain of custody — all can be challenged. Improperly obtained evidence may be suppressed, dramatically changing the outcome of your case.

📋 Records Can Be Expunged

Under Florida Statute 943.0585, qualifying arrests and charges may be expunged from your record. We help clients clear eligible offenses so past mistakes don't define their future.
